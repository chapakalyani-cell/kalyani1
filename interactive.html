What is Data Analytics?

Data Analytics is the science of examining raw data to draw conclusions and support decision-making. It combines statistics, programming, and domain expertise to uncover patterns, trends, and insights hidden in data.

From predicting customer behaviour to optimising supply chains, data analytics powers decisions across every industry.

🎯

Goal

Turn raw data into actionable insights

📐

Core Skill

Statistical thinking + domain knowledge

💡

Output

Reports, dashboards, predictions

🌐

Used In

Healthcare, finance, retail, tech & more

4 Types of Data Analytics

🔭

Descriptive

What happened? Summarises historical data — reports, dashboards, averages.

🔬

Diagnostic

Why did it happen? Drill-downs, correlations, root-cause analysis.

🔮

Predictive

What will happen? ML models, forecasting, regression.

🚀

Prescriptive

What should we do? Optimisation, simulations, recommendation engines.

Each type builds on the previous — mastering all four gives a full analytics capability.

Key Terms

Dataset▾
A structured collection of related data, typically organised in rows (records) and columns (variables). The starting point of any analysis.
KPI (Key Performance Indicator)▾
A measurable value that shows how effectively an organisation is achieving key business objectives. E.g. monthly revenue, churn rate.
ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)▾
A data integration process: Extract data from sources, Transform it to fit operational needs, and Load it into a target data warehouse.
Correlation▾
A statistical measure that describes the degree to which two variables move together. Ranges from -1 (inverse) to +1 (direct). Correlation ≠ causation.
Regression▾
A statistical method to model the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. Used for prediction and forecasting.
Data Wrangling▾
The process of cleaning, structuring, and enriching raw data into a desired format for better decision making. Also called data munging.
Dashboard▾
A visual display of the most important metrics and KPIs, consolidated onto a single screen for quick situational awareness.
Outlier▾
A data point that differs significantly from other observations. Can indicate an error or a genuinely unusual event worth investigating.
